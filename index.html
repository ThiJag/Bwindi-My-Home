Our Story

A genuine home
on the forest edge

Nestled in the hills of Kachulagenyi, just minutes from the ancient trees of Bwindi Impenetrable National Park, Bwindi My Home is a recently renovated lodge built for travellers who want authenticity alongside comfort.

With a breathtaking balcony facing the forest, a warm bar, and a garden that comes alive at dawn, this is the base camp for one of Africa's most extraordinary wildlife experiences — mountain gorilla trekking.

The Highlight

Mountain Gorilla
Trekking

Bwindi Impenetrable National Park is home to roughly half the world's remaining mountain gorilla population. From our doorstep, venture into the ancient forest for a face-to-face encounter unlike anything on Earth.

🦍
Multiple gorilla families

Several habituated gorilla families roam the Buhoma and Ruhija sectors — our team helps arrange permits well in advance.

🌿
Expert local guides

Experienced trackers and rangers lead every trek, ensuring your safety and maximum wildlife time.

📍
Prime location

Bwindi My Home sits just minutes from the park gate — no long transfers, more time in the forest.

🎒
Full trek support

Packed lunches, walking poles, gaiters, and rain gear — we prepare you for everything the rainforest has to offer.

Beyond Gorillas

Forest Experiences

🦜

Birding Walks

Bwindi hosts over 350 bird species including 23 Albertine Rift endemics. Dawn and dusk walks with expert birding guides.

🏔️

Waterfall Hikes

Jungle trails weave past cascading waterfalls and ancient trees — a meditative escape into one of Africa's oldest forests.

🏘️

Batwa Culture Tour

Meet the indigenous Batwa people, learn their forest traditions, and gain a profound perspective on life in the rainforest.

🌄

Sunrise Viewpoints

Wake before dawn and hike to panoramic ridges where mist rolls through the valley as the forest stirs to life below.

🐒

Chimpanzee Tracking

Bwindi is also home to chimpanzees and red-tailed monkeys — days in the forest rarely disappoint wildlife lovers.

🌿

Community Walks

Stroll through Buhoma village, visit local schools, and experience the warm hospitality of Ugandan highland communities.

📸

Photography

Golden hour light over the forest canopy, rare birds, charismatic primates — every moment is a frame worth capturing.

☕

Garden Relaxation

Linger over Ugandan coffee in our garden bar, let the forest soundtrack wash over you, and simply breathe it all in.

How to Find Us

Getting to Bwindi

We're located at Nyamishamba Bwindi, Kachulagenyi — on the western edge of Bwindi Impenetrable National Park. Our team can arrange transfers from Kihihi Airstrip or Entebbe.

✈️
By Air

Kihihi Airstrip — 40 km (approx. 1.5 hrs by road). Charter flights from Entebbe available. We arrange paid airport transfers.

🚗
By Road

8–9 hours from Kampala / Entebbe via Mbarara. 6 hours from Queen Elizabeth NP. Private transfers and self-drive both possible.

📍
